Finding #1

Six Judges, One Number ‚Äî Twice

Ruka Hirano received identical scores from all six judges in both Round 1 and Round 2. Every judge independently wrote down "90." This happened zero times for any other competitor. How unusual is this?

Of 24 scored performances, Ruka's two runs are the only ones where all six judges gave the exact same score. The next-tightest agreement was a spread of 1 point (Totsuka R2, Scotty R2). Wipeouts, by contrast, show spreads up to 7 points.
Finding #2

Crash Early, Score Low

When a rider wipes out, their score is almost entirely determined by one thing: how many tricks they completed before falling. The correlation is r=0.836. A rider who completes 5 tricks before crashing scores 4√ó higher than one who crashes after 2.

Judges also disagree more when scoring wipeouts ‚Äî average spread of 3.4 points vs 2.0 for clean runs. Crashes introduce subjectivity that clean landings don't.

Despite these tendencies, the scoring system's trimmed mean (drop highest and lowest) works: it shifts scores by only 0.17 pts on average, and medal rankings are identical whether you use raw or trimmed scores.
Finding #4

The Relief Bias Question

The original question: do judges score you higher if the riders right before you all crashed? After a string of wipeouts, does a clean run look extra impressive by contrast?

The between-groups difference is +1.96 pts (87.4 after crashes vs 85.4 after clean), with nearly balanced average positions. But the within-rider evidence tells a different story: Ruka Hirano scored highest (91) after zero crashes, Yamada scored identically (92) regardless, and Totsuka's +4 gain came with a trick upgrade. The data doesn't support a relief bias narrative.
Finding #5

Harder Tricks Don't Mean Higher Scores

Unlike diving or gymnastics, halfpipe has no difficulty multiplier. And the data shows it: trick difficulty barely predicts final score (r=0.195). Wang attempted the competition's hardest trick sequence (difficulty 51.5) and scored 76. Yamada did a simpler set (32.5) and scored 92.
